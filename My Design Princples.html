Andrew's Design Principles

Complexity Considerations

  • Allow users to learn the system by doing
  • Help users adopt more efficient methods
  • Provide fluid pathways
  • Reduce Clutter without losing capability
  • Make important information visually salient

Key Principles

  • Visiblity
  • Consistency
  • Feedback
  • Constraints
  • Affordance
  • Accessiblity

Human Center Design Principles

  • Put human needs behaviours first, then design to meet those needs
  • Design solutions that address real user needs and enhance their experiences
  • Must have good communication skills between designers and users to align goals and expections

Cultural Considerations

  • Don't rely on machine translation
  • Be mindful of potential cultural sensitivities and taboos.
  • Don't assume universal interpretations of color
  • Do use diverse and inclusive imagery

Cognitive Design Principles

  • Attention
    • Use techniques to achieve this when designing visual interface, such as animated graphics, color, underlinking, ordering of items, sequencing of different information, and spacing of items.
    • Avoid cluttering visual interfaces with too much information.
  • Perception
    • Design icons and other graphical representations so that users can readily distinguish
    • Design audio sounds to be readily distinguishable from one another
  • Memory
    • Reduce cognitive load by avoiding long and complicated procedures for carrying out tasks
    • Provide users with a variety of ways of labelling digital information
  • Learning
    • Design interfaces that encourages exploration and tolerate mistakes
    • Design interfaces that constrain and guide users to select appropriate actions when initially learning
  • Reading Speaking Listening
    • Keep the length of speech-based menus and instructions to a minimum.
    • Accentuate the intonation of artificially generated speech voices
  • Problem-solving, Planning, Reasoning, and Decision-making
    • Use simple and memorable functions to support rapid decision-making and planning
    • Enable users to set or save their own criteria or preferences

Conceptual Considerations

  • Be wary of a poor or erroneous model
  • Agile prioritises familiarity over novelty
  • Design the conceptual model purposely - don't let is arise from the tech
  • Make sure the mental model and the conceptional model match

Emtional/Accessibility/Errors Principles

  • Make Feedback Immediate
  • Have empathy for our users!
  • Design to reduce human errors and to prevent errors
  • Avoid overwhelming users with unnecessary error messages or alerts
